# Fraud Prevention, Abuse, and Escalation

**Document ID:** POL-FRD-001  
**Version:** 2026-01  
**Applies to:** Risk-flagged orders, repeat claims, and **pressure** for outcomes outside policy.

---

## SEC-FRD-001 — Prohibited Customer Conduct

The following may result in **account review**, **order holds**, or **law enforcement referral**:

- **Chargeback abuse** after receiving goods  
- **False** non-receipt or damage claims  
- **Social engineering** of agents to bypass policy  
- **Threats** or **harassment** toward staff  

Agents must **de-escalate** professionally and **not** negotiate under threat. Escalate to **Trust & Safety**.

---

## SEC-FRD-002 — Pressure for Unsupported Outcomes

Customers requesting **managers**, **exceptions**, or **“just this once”** resolutions **not** in policy:

- Acknowledge the request.  
- Explain **policy boundary** and **citations**.  
- Offer **escalation** to **specialist review** if **published** path exists.  
- **Do not** invent new exceptions or **confirm** outcomes not approved in system.

---

## SEC-FRD-003 — Sensitive Data

Agents and automated systems must **not** expose:

- Full **payment** or **government ID** numbers in chat  
- **Other customers’** order data  
- **Internal** fraud scores or **rule** details  

Use **masked** references only.

---

## SEC-FRD-004 — When to Abstain or Escalate

Escalate or abstain from definitive policy answers when:

- The request is **not covered** in the knowledge base.  
- **Legal** or **regulatory** interpretation is required.  
- **Conflicting** policies apply and **no** clear hierarchy (see POL-REG-001).  

Output must state **limits** and **next steps** (e.g., “specialist will respond within 24–48 hours”).

---

## SEC-FRD-005 — Documentation

Log **ticket ID**, **policy sections cited**, and **decision** for audit. **No hallucinated** policy sections in customer-facing text.
