# Returns — Perishables, Food, and Temperature-Sensitive Items

**Document ID:** POL-RET-003  
**Version:** 2026-01  
**Applies to:** Grocery, fresh, frozen, refrigerated, and items labeled perishable or temperature-controlled.

---

## SEC-PER-001 — Category Definition

**Perishable items** include but are not limited to: fresh produce, meat, seafood, dairy, bakery items with short shelf life, meal kits, and items requiring cold chain. **Item category** in order metadata must be used to apply this document.

---

## SEC-PER-002 — Quality Issues vs. Preference

- **Spoilage, contamination, or temperature failure** documented at delivery (photos, carrier notes): Eligible for **full refund or replacement** per POL-DIS-001; return of physical product may **not** be required if disposal is safer.
- **“Did not like taste”** or **texture preference** after opening: Generally **not** refundable unless the product was **mislabeled** or **not as described**.

Melting of heat-sensitive items (e.g., chocolate) due to **carrier delay beyond our SLA** may qualify for partial or full refund; melting due to **customer delay in retrieval** after delivery notification may not.

---

## SEC-PER-003 — Return of Physical Product

Unopened perishables in **salable condition** may be returned within **48 hours** of delivery for refund only if:

- Packaging is intact and cold chain was maintained, and  
- Local regulations allow resale.

Opened perishable food is **not accepted** for return except for verified safety or mis-shipment cases.

---

## SEC-PER-004 — Subscription and Recurring Orders

Skipped or modified delivery windows follow **subscription terms** shown at signup. Refunds for duplicate or unwanted subscription shipments depend on whether the customer **confirmed** the upcoming shipment in the account portal.

---

## SEC-PER-005 — Documentation Requirements

Agents may request:

- Photos of packaging and product  
- Lot or expiry information if visible  
- Delivery timestamp vs. first complaint time  

**Do not** promise refunds without verifying against carrier and fulfillment logs when the dispute involves timing or temperature.
